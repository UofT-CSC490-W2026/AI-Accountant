  [PROCEED] capitalization vs. expense treatment of marketing campaign
    The transaction states Mondelez 'commissioned a digital marketing campaign' and paid for it. This language indicates purchasing campaign services (advertising, media placement, creative execution) rather than acquiring identifiable intangible assets. Under IAS 38.69, advertising and promotional expenditure is recognized as an expense when the entity has the right to access the goods or receives the services. Capitalization requires clear evidence of an identifiable asset with controlled future benefits. The transaction text provides no indication of asset creation (no mention of purchased databases, owned content, or developed IP). Following the accounting convention that advertising is expensed unless there is specific evidence of asset acquisition, the expense treatment is appropriate.
Rationale: Marketing campaign commissioned and paid in cash. Under IFRS (IAS 38), most advertising and promotional expenditures are expensed as incurred unless they create a separately identifiable intangible asset with probable future economic benefits. The term 'commissioned a digital marketing campaign' typically refers to purchasing advertising services (media buys, campaign execution, creative services) rather than acquiring owned digital assets. Without evidence of identifiable assets being created (e.g., purchased customer databases, owned digital content rights, developed brand assets), the default treatment is to expense.
